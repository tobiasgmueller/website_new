Pathogen induced Cannibalism

As an undergraduate researcher I worked for 3 years along Jay Rosenheim studing the insect communities of alfalfa and cotton in California's Central Valley. We had observed that the generalist insect predator Geocoris pallens, a previously ubiquitous member of the community, was becoming increasingly hard to find during field surveys. At the same time we observed a increase rate of filial cannibalism, where a mother eats her own eggs.

Our survey work, coupled with labratory assays of the prevelance of filial cannibalism, showed that the frequency and severity of the cannbalism trail correlated temporally with the observed collapse in the Geocoris population.

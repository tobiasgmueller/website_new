Floral nectar Microbiomes

So much information and fun facts will eventually be here about nectar microbes! Exciting stuff eh?!? Floral nectar is frequently colonized by fungi and bacteria. However, within individual flowers, nectar microbial communities are typically species-poor and dominated by few cosmopolitan genera. One hypothesis is that nectar constituents may act as a strong environmental filter. Non-sugar constituents in nectar could affect species composition via broad antimicrobial activity or differential effects on nectar microbial species.

My work focused on microbial specific impacts of nectar chemistries assaying across a gradient of nectar specialist microbes, to envirinmental and bee associated microbes

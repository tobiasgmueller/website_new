Hi! My name is Tobias. I'm an entomology PhD student at Cornell university. Currently working on solitary bees and their brood cell microbial communities

Im fascinated by insect microbial interactions - especially the processes that impact community formation and form. Before coming to Cornell I worked with Rachel Vannette at UC Davis studying microbial communities in floral nectar and with Jay Rosenheim studying community dynamics in cotton and alfalfa systems as well as early season insect pests of California citrus

Solitary bee microbial communities

I am currently working to better understand solitary bee brood cell microbial community formation with a special interest in factors that lead to or inhibit pathogen establishment

Nectar Microbes and Chemistry

I previously researched how nectar secondary compounds impacts community formation to better understand the drivers of low diversity of nectar microbial communities

Citrus IPM

I researched early season citrus pests and found that mandarin oranges have natural resistance against several early season citrus pests including thrips and katydids

Pathogen Induced Cannibalism

We observed the population collapse of a gerealist insect predator and found it was correlated to high level of pathogen induced filial cannibalism

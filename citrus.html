Early season IPM in Citrus

Mandarins and other quick peel citrus are quickly taking over the citrus industry and is one of the most planted cultivars. However mandarins are a relatively recent development and management guidelines and spray regimes currently in place were created in the 1980s to apply to sweet oranges. I worked for alongside Hanna Kahl, Bodil Cass, and Jay Rosenheim to assess how different mandarin and orange cultivars differed in their pest pressures looking primarily at katydids, earwigs, and thrips.

We found all sorts of cool things like katydid damage on clementines forming unusual scarring that was being misdiagnosed at caterpillar damage, both katydids and thrips avoiding mandarin (C. reticulata) and damaging them less, and earwigs being described as a novel citrus pest!
